    What is an Exponent?

    An exponent refers to how many times a number (the base) is multiplied by itself. For example, 23 (read as "2 to the power of 3") means 2 × 2 × 2 = 8.

    Basic Rules of Exponents:

    • Base1: Any number raised to the power of 1 is the number itself. Example: 51 = 5.
    • Base0: Any number raised to the power of 0 is 1. Example: 70 = 1.
    • Base-n: Negative exponents give the reciprocal of the base raised to the positive exponent. Example: 2-3 = 1/23 = 1/8.

    Exponents are used to simplify large numbers. For example, 106 means 1,000,000.
